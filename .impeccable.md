# Impeccable Design Context — wrksourcing.com

## Design Context

### Users
Entrepreneurs and SMB owners (1-20 people, $500K-$5M revenue), primarily Canadian. They are busy, overwhelmed by operations, and skeptical of "VA agencies" that overpromise. They land on the site during a moment of operational pain: hiring is slow, systems are broken, admin is eating their time. They need to feel that wrksourcing understands their problem and has a real system to fix it, not just warm bodies to throw at it.

### Brand Personality
**Grounded, capable, approachable.** Not corporate. Not startup-flashy. Not a typical VA agency. wrksourcing is the calm, competent operations partner who shows up, audits the mess, builds the systems, and deploys trained people. The brand speaks like a peer who has done this before, not a vendor trying to sell.

### Emotional Goals
When a potential client lands on wrksourcing.com, they should feel:
- "These people are competent and will actually fix my operations"
- "This is a real company with real systems, not a freelance marketplace"
- "I can trust them with my business"
- Relief that someone understands the operational chaos they are in

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Minimal, clean, professional warmth. High contrast. Intentional white space
- **Theme:** Light mode primary. Dark forest (#253530) sections for contrast and gravitas
- **Color system:** Mantis gradient (#76d669 → #DDEA7F) as the singular accent. Not scattered colors. One gradient, used with discipline
- **Motion:** Restrained and purposeful. Scroll reveals fire once. No bounce or elastic easing. Spring physics for natural feel. Nothing flashy for the sake of flashy
- **Glass effects:** Frosted glass (backdrop-blur) for depth layering, used sparingly
- **Typography:** Avenir Heavy for authority in headers, Avenir Book for readable body. Plus Jakarta Sans as the web fallback. No Inter, no system defaults

### Anti-References (What This Must NOT Look Like)
- Not a VA agency site (no stock photos of headsets, no "hire a virtual assistant" language)
- Not a corporate consulting firm (no navy blue, no suits, no buzzword bingo)
- Not a flashy startup (no purple gradients, no "disrupting" anything, no 3D illustrations)
- Not a freelance marketplace (no gig economy aesthetic, no "find talent fast")
- Not generic AI-generated UI (no Inter font, no cards-in-cards, no gray-on-color text, no pure blacks)

### Design Principles
1. **Systems first, people second.** The design should communicate structured competence. Grids, clear hierarchy, consistent spacing. The visual language mirrors the service: we build systems
2. **One accent, used with discipline.** The mantis gradient is the brand. It appears on CTAs, highlights, and moments of emphasis. Everything else is neutral. No color scatter
3. **Earned trust through craft.** Small details (smooth animations, precise alignment, thoughtful hover states) signal that wrksourcing pays attention to details, the same way they will with a client's operations
4. **Warmth without softness.** The tone is human and approachable but never casual or playful. Warm like a confident handshake, not warm like a hug
5. **Show, don't decorate.** Every visual element serves communication. No ornamental gradients, no decorative blobs, no floating shapes. If it does not help the visitor understand or trust, it does not belong

### Technical Constraints
- No Tailwind (inline styles + CSS variables)
- Avenir is a local font (not hosted), falls back to Plus Jakarta Sans from Google Fonts
- Framer Motion for all animation (no CSS-only animations for entrance effects)
- Next.js 14 App Router, all pages are client components currently
- Max-width 1200px container, 2rem horizontal padding
- Responsive breakpoints: 900px (tablet), 600px (mobile)
- Brand colors defined as CSS custom properties in globals.css
